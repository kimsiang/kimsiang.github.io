// Research

Pushing the Precision Frontier

From fundamental symmetries to underground imaging — our experiments span the full spectrum of muon physics.

Muon g−2

Precision measurement of the muon's anomalous magnetic moment at Fermilab and J-PARC — one of the most sensitive tests of the Standard Model and a window to new physics beyond it.

Fermilab E989J-PARC E34Anomalous Precession

Muon EDM

Searching for the muon's electric dipole moment at PSI using the frozen-spin technique — probing CP violation and matter-antimatter asymmetry with sensitivity to 10⁻²³ e·cm.

PSI muEDMCP ViolationFrozen-Spin

SHINE Muon Source

Establishing a high-repetition-rate pulsed muon source at the SHINE facility using an 8 GeV CW superconducting RF linac — building China's next-generation muon beam for μSR and particle physics.

SHINE FacilityBeam R&DμSR

Muography

Imaging underground structures with cosmic-ray muons — developing next-generation detectors and applying muon tomography to survey city infrastructure like Shanghai's Outer Ring Tunnel.

Detector R&DUrban ImagingSiPM / MWPC
